Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026 · MemoRebel v1.0

Data We Collect

  • GPS coordinates (when you pin memories, record routes, or start a study walk)
  • Location name (reverse-geocoded, stored locally)
  • Memory notes and photos (stored locally only)
  • Habit check-in times
  • Study session progress (distance, quiz answers)
  • Mood logs (emotion type, intensity, optional note, location)
  • Device-generated streak data

We do not collect: payment data, or device identifiers beyond what Android OS provides for standard app operation.

How We Use Your Data

All data is used exclusively to power app features you actively use: GPS for memory pinning, route recording, geofencing, and study checkpoints; study data for progress tracking; mood/habit data for the Life Mood Map and Habit Garden; streak data for your Home screen and achievements. Data is never sold or used for advertising.

Data Storage

Personal data is stored locally on your device (Room database). If you sign in with Google, memories, routes, stats, achievements, and habits are additionally synced to a private Firestore document scoped to your account so you can restore them on a new device. Gemini/Groq AI prompts sent from the app contain your study topic (e.g. "French Revolution") but no personal identifying information, coordinates, or memory content.

Friends & Social Features

If you use the Friends feature, your display name, email, and a public MemoRebel ID become visible to other users so they can find and add you. Friend requests, your friends list, and any routes/achievements you choose to share with friends are stored in Firestore. You can remove a friend, block a user, or report a user for inappropriate behavior at any time from the Friends screen — see our Community Guidelines.

Third-Party Services

  • Google Maps SDK — map display and GPS.
  • Groq / Google Gemini AI — study plan and story generation. Only the topic text is sent, never personal data.
  • Wikipedia REST API — content fetching, no personal data sent.
  • Google Play Services & Firebase (Auth, Firestore) — sign-in and optional cloud sync.

None of these services receive your memories, mood logs, photos, or personal notes.

Crash Reports

To help us fix bugs, MemoRebel can send a device model, Android version, app version, and stack trace after a crash. This is opt-out and can be disabled anytime in Settings → Diagnostics → Share Crash Reports. No personal content (memories, notes, photos) is ever included in a crash report.

Your Rights (India DPDP Act 2023)

You may request access to, correction of, or erasure of your data. Since most data is stored locally, you can exercise these rights directly via Android Settings → Apps → MemoRebel → Clear Data, or by deleting your account from the app. For assistance, email us below.
